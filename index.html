A new Educational Initiative for practical learning

2D Animation 10mins ~ 6-11

The Bouba and Zaza animated series follows the journey of two young characters, Bouba and Zaza - a boy and a girl, who learn through lively stories about caring for the environment, understanding HIV and AIDS, accepting people’s differences and other themes in direct resonance with contemporary society. Well beyond simply providing much needed reading materials, the series encourages a dialogue with teachers, caregivers, and parents around a whole range of civic issues.

As part of a number of initiatives set up to support a new global coalition to protect the right to education during unprecedented times, the UNESCO, Michel Lafon Education and the Association for the Development of Education in Africa (ADEA) are working towards the development of an educational project that focuses on educating children through edutainment across Africa, as part of a global response to COVID-19.

The Bouba & Zaza educational series, titled “Childhood Cultures”, is a collection of 12 story books and short films, which educate children on challenging themes that are not necessarily addressed within the home or part of the education curriculum across Africa.

The Twelve Themes

Edutainment – Why is it needed?

As a response and approach to addressing the rising social issues in communities which are due to the impact of COVID-19, the project will move to the development and roll out of 12animated films based on the collection of themes. This daring collection approaches sensitive themes such as sexual abuse in the home, access to clean water and much more; with the purpose of breaking taboos and bringing an awareness during this time where children are more vulnerable. This educational initiative will be delivered to children across Africa through the distribution of visually stunning & story-driven animation on the variety of themes and distributed through a variety of platforms. We believe you will agree with us that this is an important cause in providing education to children during this unprecedented time and will create an impact of change at grassroots level and as an educational response to COVID-19.

With this belief, Michel Lafon Education and UNESCO are seeking to partner with individuals, organisations, and the private sector – committed to the development of educating children in Africa as part of the UNESCO global education coalition.
